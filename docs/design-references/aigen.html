Card styles
Concept Term → Definition
The front names a concept. The back gives a clear, self-contained definition. Best for vocabulary, terminology, and building foundational knowledge.
Example
F Gradient descent B An optimization algorithm that iteratively adjusts parameters in the direction of steepest decrease of the loss function.
Q&A Question → Answer
The front asks a specific question targeting one fact. The back gives a concise, direct answer. Best for testing understanding and application.
Example
F Why do transformers use positional encoding? B Because self-attention is permutation-invariant — it has no built-in notion of token order.
Cloze Fill in the blank
The front is a sentence with a key term replaced by a blank. The back gives the missing term plus a short explanation. Best for memorizing specific facts, names, and values.
Example
F The scaling factor in attention is 1 / √ B dₖ — the dimension of the key vectors. Prevents dot products from growing too large.
